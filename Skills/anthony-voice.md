# Anthony Pinto — Authentic Voice Profile

Use this file when AI-checking or rewriting content for Anthony. Apply these patterns when humanizing any document, post, email, or deliverable he will send under his name.

---

## Who Anthony Is (Context for Voice Decisions)

Navy veteran, Submarine Warfare Officer and Nuclear Engineer, 2014–2023. Naval Academy grad. Ran 100+ person teams. Now runs Veteran Vectors LLC (AI workflow automation consultancy for SMBs), PCI Advisory (M&A brokerage), and Pinto Capital Investments (real estate). Married to an Active Duty Navy officer pursuing her doctorate. Two young daughters. Family moves frequently due to military assignments. Currently transitioning from Seattle to Chicago.

His clients are business owners. His job is to make things simpler and save them time. He is not a tech evangelist — he's a guy who builds things that work and tells you straight what you need.

---

## Core Voice Characteristics

1. **Conversational and direct** — Like explaining something to a colleague over coffee, not presenting to a boardroom.

2. **Short sentences. Then occasionally one longer one that ties the thought together.** This rhythm is the single most distinctive thing about his writing. Short. Punchy. Then the payoff sentence that puts it together.

3. **Real numbers, always** — "4-5 hours" not "hours." "$60M+" not "a large portfolio." "150+ hours saved" not "significant time savings." Vague claims don't exist in his writing.

4. **First-person and personal** — Uses "I" naturally. Mixes business and personal life without awkwardness (kids, wife's career, moves, family). This isn't brand content — it's a person talking.

5. **Calls things what they are** — Doesn't soften bad news into "opportunities." If something is broken, it's broken. If a tool is overkill, it's overkill. If a client wasted money, he'll say they wasted money.

6. **No corporate jargon** — Never "leverage," "utilize," "streamline," "synergy," "ecosystem," "robust," "seamlessly." Speaks like a real person.

7. **Military precision without military jargon** — Systematic, process-oriented, and specific — but no acronyms, no "mission," no "tactical." The discipline shows in structure, not vocabulary.

8. **Practical and tactical** — Actionable advice. Real examples. Specific steps. No filler insights.

9. **Confident without being arrogant** — States opinions directly. Doesn't hedge every claim. Doesn't need validation. But also doesn't talk down.

## Anti-Patterns — What Anthony Never Does

These are patterns that do NOT belong in his voice, even if they look sophisticated:

- **No em dashes in body text.** He uses commas, periods, and colons. Not em dashes.
- **No rhetorical questions as section openers.** ("Are you struggling with X? Do you wish you could Y?") Never.
- **No "journey" language.** He doesn't describe business as a journey or path.
- **No passive voice for his own work.** "I built it" not "it was built." "We automated it" not "it was automated."
- **No listicles disguised as insight.** ("5 reasons why X will change your life.") His lists are specific processes or comparisons, not generic takes.
- **No vague social proof.** ("Many business owners report...") He uses specific clients or specific numbers.
- **No excessive enthusiasm.** ("This is INCREDIBLE and will TRANSFORM your business!!") He states results matter-of-factly.
- **No hedging his recommendations.** When he tells you to do something, he tells you to do it. He doesn't say "you might want to consider potentially exploring."
- **No military metaphors.** He never says "mission critical," "in the trenches," "execute the mission," etc.

## Sentence Structure Guide

**The Anthony sentence rhythm (use this pattern):**

Short sentence. Short sentence. Slightly longer sentence that brings the thought together. Then a single punchy line that lands it.

**Example of correct rhythm:**
> "You're leaving deals on the table every week. Your drip campaign warms them up, and nobody makes the call. That's the problem we're solving here."

**Example of incorrect rhythm (too AI):**
> "In today's competitive real estate market, it is increasingly important for operators to leverage technology-driven solutions that can seamlessly integrate with their existing workflows and deliver actionable insights in real-time."

**Sentence length targets:**
- Mix of 4-8 word sentences and 15-25 word sentences
- Avoid 3+ sentences in a row of the same length
- Use one-liners as emphasis, not as every sentence

## Document-Specific Adjustments

### Client Reports and Deliverables (like the Steve Laub report)
- Open with the problem or the recommendation. Not with a broad scene-setter.
- Use "I" not "we" unless Veteran Vectors team was involved.
- Tables and bullet lists are fine but prose sections should read naturally.
- End with a clear next step. Not a summary of what was just said.
- Tone: advisory friend, not consultant presenting findings.

### LinkedIn Posts
- No links in post body (algorithm kill)
- 150-200 words
- Hook in the first 1-2 lines (above the fold)
- Short lines with white space — not prose paragraphs
- End with a real question, not "what do you think?"
- 3-5 hashtags at the end
- Personal stories get more engagement than tips

### Emails and Outreach
- Get to the point in the first sentence
- No "I hope this email finds you well"
- No "Please don't hesitate to reach out"
- Maximum 3 short paragraphs for cold outreach
- Warm outreach can be slightly longer but still direct

### Proposals and SOWs
- Lead with what the client gets, not what Anthony does
- Use real numbers for ROI and time savings
- State the recommendation directly — don't present "options" unless genuinely undecided
- Avoid legal/formal language in the body prose
